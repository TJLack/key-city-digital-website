## 11) Funnel Logic: When to Push Leak Detector vs Booking Calendar

## A) Traffic-source + intent routing

### Push to **WebsiteLeakDetector.com first** when users are:
- Cold traffic from SEO/blog/social
- Early-stage and “problem aware” but not vendor-ready
- Comparing options or unsure what service they need
- Landing on industries pages or informational service content

**Reason:** lower-friction micro-conversion; creates diagnostic context for sales.

### Push **directly to booking calendar** when users are:
- High-intent (visited 2+ service pages, results page, pricing/consult pages)
- Returning users
- Referral traffic from warm introductions
- Paid ad traffic with consultation-focused campaign intent
- On contact page and bottom-of-page CTA sections

**Reason:** reduce steps for ready-to-buy prospects.

## B) On-page CTA hierarchy by page type

- **Home/Services/Industries/City pages:**
  - Hero CTA #1 = Leak Detector
  - Hero CTA #2 = Book Consultation
  - Mid-page CTA = Leak Detector
  - Bottom CTA = Book Consultation

- **Results/About/High-intent service pages (Ads, AI, Branding):**
  - Hero CTA #1 = Book Consultation
  - Hero CTA #2 = Leak Detector

- **Contact page:**
  - Primary = Embedded booking calendar
  - Secondary = Leak Detector link for not-yet-ready users

## C) Simple lead scoring trigger suggestions (for CRM/automation)

- **Score +10**: completed leak detector
- **Score +15**: viewed 2+ service pages
- **Score +20**: viewed results page
- **Score +30**: reached contact page
- **Score +50**: booked consultation

**Automation rule:** If score ≥ 40 and not booked, trigger booking-focused follow-up CTA sequence.
